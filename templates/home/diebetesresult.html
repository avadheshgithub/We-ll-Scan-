Tests Recommended

HbA1C

A1C blood test measures glucose
attached to hemoglobin, reflecting
average blood sugar over months.
6.5% or higher signals diabetes,
5.7-6.4% indicates prediabetes,
below 5.7% is normal.

Blood Sugar Test

The fasting blood sugar test
requires an overnight fast.
Less than 100 mg/dL is normal,
100-125 mg/dL indicates prediabetes,
and 126 mg/dL or higher on two
tests signifies diabetes.

Cholesterol Test

Diabetes heightens heart disease
risk, necessitating frequent
cholesterol screening.
High cholesterol warrants closer
monitoring due to increased
cardiovascular risks.

Triglycerides

Triglycerides, fats in blood,
elevate heart disease risk.
Normal: below 150; high: above 200.
Causes include obesity, inactivity,
smoking, alcohol, genetics.
Lower via weight loss,
healthy diet, exercise.

Creatinine Blood Test

Creatinine blood test gauges
creatinine levels,
a muscle waste product.
Elevated levels may indicate
kidney function.
It provides insights to your
doctor about renal health.

Electrolytes

Electrolyte blood test assesses
sodium, potassium, chloride, and
bicarbonate levels. It evaluating
heart disease symptoms and
monitoring treatment for condition
like high blood pressure,
heart failure, and
liver/kidney diseases..
